# Team Operations Guide

## Daily Dashboard Review

### Morning SLO Check

Start each day with a 5-minute dashboard review to catch potential issues early:

**Essential Daily Metrics:**
- Current SLO achievement rate (target: 95%)
- Tickets at risk of SLO breach (>80% of target time elapsed)
- Service Quality percentage and trends
- Throughput compared to team capacity

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- SLO performance below 90% (warning threshold)
- Multiple tickets approaching SLO deadline
- Unusual spikes in ticket volume or resolution times
- Service Quality declining below 90%

**Immediate Actions:**
- Prioritize at-risk tickets for same-day resolution
- Reassign overloaded team members' tickets
- Escalate tickets requiring external dependencies
- Document any systemic issues for weekly review

### Ongoing Performance Monitoring

**Individual Level:**
- Track personal SLO contribution and improvement trends
- Monitor assigned ticket progress against deadlines
- Identify tickets requiring assistance or escalation

**Team Level:**
- Monitor team velocity and capacity utilization
- Track distribution of work across team members
- Identify workflow bottlenecks and dependencies

## Weekly Operations

### Team Meeting Integration

**Weekly SLO Review (15 minutes):**
1. **Performance Summary:** Week-over-week SLO trends
2. **Quality Analysis:** Service Quality and reopening rates
3. **Throughput Review:** Completed tickets vs. target capacity
4. **Issue Resolution:** Tickets missed SLO and root causes
5. **Action Planning:** Specific improvements for next week

**Key Discussion Points:**
- Which issue types consistently miss/meet SLO targets
- Resource reallocation needs based on performance data
- Process improvements identified through SLO analysis
- External factors affecting team performance

### Performance Trend Analysis

**Weekly Analysis Checklist:**
- [ ] Compare 4-week SLO performance trend
- [ ] Review lead time, cycle time, and response time patterns
- [ ] Analyze throughput patterns and capacity utilization
- [ ] Check Service Quality metrics and rework indicators
- [ ] Identify seasonal or cyclical performance patterns
- [ ] Validate SLO targets remain business-appropriate

## Managing SLO Performance

### When SLOs Are Being Missed

**Immediate Response (24-48 hours):**
1. **Triage:** Prioritize highest-risk tickets for immediate attention
2. **Resource Shift:** Temporarily reallocate team members to critical tickets
3. **Stakeholder Alert:** Notify customers of potential delays
4. **Quick Resolution:** Clear any tickets that can be completed rapidly

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- Review status transition bottlenecks
- Analyze resource constraints and skill gaps
- Examine external dependency delays
- Assess process efficiency opportunities

**Improvement Actions:**
- Implement workflow optimizations
- Request additional resources if consistently overcapacity
- Negotiate SLO adjustments if targets prove unrealistic
- Enhance team training in identified skill gaps

### When SLOs Are Consistently Exceeded

**Optimization Strategies:**
- **Target Refinement:** Consider tightening SLO targets for continuous improvement
- **Capacity Reallocation:** Shift resources to underperforming areas
- **Best Practice Sharing:** Document and teach successful approaches
- **Advanced Services:** Consider expanding service offerings

## Team Communication and Escalation

### Performance Transparency

**Regular Communication:**
- Share monthly SLO performance summaries with team
- Recognize individual and team SLO achievements
- Provide context for any performance challenges
- Maintain open dialogue about improvement opportunities

**Real-Time Updates:**
- Use team channels for immediate SLO alerts
- Include SLO highlights in daily standup meetings
- Share progress on improvement initiatives

### Escalation Procedures

**Escalation Triggers:**
- SLO performance below 85% for two consecutive weeks
- Service Quality below 85% indicating systemic quality issues
- Throughput declining more than 20% without clear cause
- Customer complaints related to service delivery times

**Escalation Process:**
1. Document specific SLO impacts and contributing factors
2. Propose concrete solutions and resource requirements
3. Notify capability owner and management chain
4. Establish regular follow-up schedule until resolution

## Process Improvement Through SLO Insights

### Performance Analysis

**Bottleneck Identification:**
- Analyze status transition times to find delays
- Review common characteristics of SLO-missed tickets
- Examine resource utilization during peak periods
- Map dependencies causing frequent delays

**Data-Driven Improvements:**
- Use reopening rates to identify quality gaps
- Analyze lead time patterns to optimize intake processes
- Review cycle time data to streamline workflows
- Monitor response time trends to set realistic expectations

### Continuous Enhancement

**Monthly Improvements:**
- Implement one process enhancement based on SLO data
- Measure improvement impact on subsequent performance
- Share successful improvements with other teams
- Document lessons learned for future reference

**Strategic Optimization:**
- Quarterly review of SLO targets and business alignment
- Annual assessment of service offerings and capacity
- Cross-capability benchmarking for best practice adoption
- Long-term automation roadmap based on repetitive SLO challenges

## Success Metrics and Recognition

**Team Success Indicators:**
- Sustained SLO achievement above 90%
- Month-over-month improvement in Service Quality
- Consistent throughput meeting capacity targets
- Reduced escalations and customer complaints

**Recognition Programs:**
- Celebrate teams achieving 95%+ SLO performance
- Highlight innovative improvements in team meetings
- Share success stories across capabilities
- Acknowledge individual contributions to SLO achievement

By integrating SLO monitoring into daily operations, teams can maintain high performance standards while continuously optimizing their service delivery processes. The key is making SLO awareness a natural part of workflow management rather than an additional reporting burden.
