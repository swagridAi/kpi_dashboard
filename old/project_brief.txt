You are building a Service Level Objective (SLO) KPI dashboard in Power BI for a centralized data team within a large investment bank. The team currently offers services across:

Data Quality
Data Extracts
Change Controls
Reference Data
Records Management

The team is focused on financial data products but is expanding into non-product domains by abstracting its work into capabilities that can be offered across the organization. The Jira → Database ingestion is already working, and Power BI Premium licensing is available.
Key Organizational Objectives
1. Scalable Operating Model

Self-Service Governance: Enable capability owners to maintain their own configurations without IT dependency
Federated Ownership: Support multiple teams owning different capabilities while maintaining centralized standards
Change Management: Version-controlled processes for mapping updates and SLO adjustments with proper approval workflows

2. Cross-Organizational Adoption

Standardized Service Framework: Establish consistent measurement and reporting across all capabilities
Internal Customer Onboarding: Reduce friction for teams adopting centralized data services
Business Value Communication: Clear articulation of service value to drive adoption and investment

3. Sustainable Operations

Capability Center of Excellence: Build expertise and standards that can scale across the organization
Knowledge Management: Embedded documentation and training materials accessible within the dashboard
Continuous Improvement: Built-in analytics to identify optimization opportunities and service gaps

Organizational Structure & Responsibilities
Centralized Data Team (Dashboard Owners)

Platform Management: Maintain dashboard infrastructure and core functionality
Standards & Governance: Establish organization-wide SLO frameworks and measurement standards
Training & Support: Provide guidance to capability owners and internal customers

Capability Owners (Federated Teams)

Service Definition: Define what constitutes their capability and service boundaries
SLO Target Setting: Establish realistic and business-aligned performance targets
Configuration Management: Maintain ticket-type mappings and status definitions for their services
Performance Accountability: Own the improvement of their capability's performance metrics

Internal Customers (Service Consumers)

Service Utilization: Submit tickets and consume services through standardized processes
Feedback & Requirements: Provide input on service quality and feature requirements
Self-Service Access: Use dashboard for service status, performance insights, and escalations

Organizational Governance Model
Configuration Change Process

All configuration tables will be maintained directly in Confluence, with structured tables embedded in pages owned by each capability team. This eliminates the need for a separate configuration interface while providing familiar collaboration tools for change management.

Capability Owner Proposal: Make configuration changes directly in their assigned Confluence page sections
Peer Review: Other capability owners can review and provide feedback
Standards Validation: Centralized team validates against organizational standards
Business Approval: Stakeholder approval for SLO target changes
Audit Trail: All changes logged with business justification and approver

Performance Accountability Framework

Regular Reviews: Monthly capability owner sessions to review performance and identify blockers
Escalation Paths: Defined processes for persistent SLO breaches
Improvement Plans: Collaborative development of action plans for underperforming capabilities
Recognition Programs: Highlight high-performing teams and improvement successes

Scalability Features for Organizational Growth
1. Multi-Tenant Architecture

Capability Isolation: Each team can customize their view while maintaining organizational consistency
Role-Based Access: Granular permissions for different organizational levels
Branded Capability Views: Teams can add custom branding while maintaining overall platform look

2. Self-Service Configuration

Confluence-Based Configuration Management: Capability owners maintain their configurations directly in structured Confluence tables, utilizing familiar wiki editing capabilities
Validation Rules: Automated checks during nightly pipeline execution, with validation results posted back to Confluence
Change History & Rollback: Leverage Confluence's native page versioning for audit trails and configuration rollback to previous versions

3. Organizational Knowledge Base

Embedded Learning: Contextual help and best practices within the dashboard
Success Stories: Share improvement case studies across teams
Community Features: Discussion forums and knowledge sharing between capability owners
