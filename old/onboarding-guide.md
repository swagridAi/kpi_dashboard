# SLO Dashboard Onboarding Guide for Teams

This guide explains how to get started with the Service Level Objective (SLO) dashboard in simple terms. It outlines what your team needs to do, what you'll receive, and how long the process takes.

## What You'll Get

After onboarding, your team will have:

* **Performance Dashboards**: Clear visualizations of your team's service delivery performance
* **Automated Reporting**: Monthly emails with your key metrics and trends
* **Proactive Alerts**: Early warnings before SLO breaches occur
* **Self-Service Configuration**: The ability to update your own settings through Confluence
* **Historical Trending**: Six-month performance history to identify patterns
* **Accountability Tools**: Clear metrics to demonstrate your team's value
* **Process Insights**: Identification of bottlenecks and improvement opportunities

## What You Need to Do

### 1. Request Onboarding
* Email platform.support@company.com to express interest
* Provide your team name, primary contact, and brief description of services
* Schedule an initial kickoff meeting

### 2. Define Your Capability (1 week)
* Complete the Capability Definition template in Confluence
* List and describe the services your team provides
* Identify who will be your Capability Owner and Team Lead

### 3. Map Your Workflow (1-2 weeks)
* Document your ticket statuses and how they flow
* Define which statuses mark the start/end of lead time, cycle time, and response time
* Identify any special rules (like excluding weekends or pausing on waiting states)

### 4. Set Performance Targets (1 week)
* Establish realistic SLO targets for lead time, cycle time, and response time
* Document business justification for your targets
* Get stakeholder agreement on your proposed targets

### 5. Complete Training (1 day)
* Attend dashboard training session
* Learn how to configure alerts and subscriptions
* Understand how to update your configurations

### 6. Fine-Tune and Go Live (1-2 weeks)
* Review initial metrics and make any needed adjustments
* Set up team member access permissions
* Complete final verification and signoff

## Timeline and Effort

The entire process typically takes 4-6 weeks:

| Phase | Duration | Main Effort Required From |
|-------|----------|---------------------------|
| Request and Kickoff | 1 day | Capability Owner |
| Capability Definition | 2-3 days | Capability Owner + Team Lead |
| Service Mapping | 3-5 days | Service Managers + Subject Matter Experts |
| Status Workflow Analysis | 3-5 days | Team Lead + Subject Matter Experts |
| SLO Target Setting | 3-5 days | Capability Owner + Service Managers |
| Configuration Validation | 1-2 days | Team Lead + Subject Matter Experts |
| Training | 1 day | All team members |
| Fine-Tuning | 10-15 days | Service Managers + Subject Matter Experts |
| Final Signoff | 1 day | Capability Owner + Team Lead |

Most teams can complete the process within 4-6 weeks, with the most time-intensive activities being service mapping, workflow analysis, and SLO target setting.

## Who Does What

### From Your Team:
* **Capability Owner**: Provides final approval, sets performance expectations
* **Team Lead**: Manages implementation, ensures team participation
* **Service Managers**: Define services and recommend SLO targets
* **Subject Matter Experts**: Provide workflow details and validate configurations

### From the Support Team:
* **Platform Manager**: Processes your request and schedules kickoff
* **Configuration Specialist**: Helps set up your Confluence pages
* **Data Analyst**: Configures your metrics and dashboards
* **Trainer**: Delivers training sessions for your team
* **Change Management Specialist**: Ensures proper governance
